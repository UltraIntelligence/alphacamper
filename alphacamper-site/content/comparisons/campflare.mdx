# Campflare is free. Alphacamper solves a different problem.

Campflare is easy to like. It gives people free alerts, it lowers the barrier to trying cancellation monitoring, and for a lot of campers that is enough. If your goal is "tell me when something opens," Campflare is a real answer.

Alphacamper is for the next problem. Not getting the alert. Winning the race after the alert.

## What Campflare does well

Campflare made free campsite alerts a normal expectation, which is good for campers. It is easy to try. There is very little risk in signing up and seeing whether an alert lands for your park or date.

That makes it especially good for casual users, price-sensitive users, or people who just want another line in the water without paying first. Their Hipcamp connection also makes the free-alert story even easier to understand.

For plenty of campers, that is enough value by itself.

## What Campflare does not do

Campflare still leaves you with the hardest part once the opening appears.

You still have to race to the booking page, log in, fill the routine fields, and submit before someone else does. If you are already losing campsites in the final booking flow, free alerts do not remove that bottleneck. They just tell you when the bottleneck started.

That is the whole distinction between these products.

## What Alphacamper does differently

Alphacamper also watches for cancellations. We also notify you when a site opens. But we add the piece Campflare does not:

- live cancellation detection
- SMS notifications
- a Chrome extension that pre-fills your booking form

The extension runs in your browser and fills the repetitive fields fast. That is it. It is not magic. It is not a guarantee. It is just a real speed advantage in the part of the booking flow where people usually lose.

If you have ever said, "I got the alert and still missed it," this is the product difference that matters.

## When Campflare is the right choice

If you mainly want free alerts, Campflare is the right answer. Full stop. There is no reason to pay Alphacamper just to replicate something you can already get for free.

Campflare is also a good fit if you are happy booking manually, you are chasing less competitive trips, or you simply want a zero-cost way to keep an eye on openings.

## When Alphacamper is the right choice

Alphacamper is for people who keep losing despite having the alert. Families with one important annual trip. Customers who do not want to spend the whole summer staring at their phone and then scrambling through a form every time a site opens.

If your issue is speed after the alert, Alphacamper is the better fit. If your issue is just "please notify me for free," Campflare probably is.
